02. The Science of Improvement

Quality Improvement (QI) is not just "trying harder." It is a formal science that combines clinical epidemiology, social psychology, and systems engineering.

The Seven Propositions (Perla et al., 2013)

P1: Aim

Science is guided by an explicit purpose.

P2: Context

Local context is a crucial variable in outcomes.

P3: Integration

Integrates different types of knowledge.

P4: Iteration

Uses iterative learning (PDSA cycles).

P5: Language

Requires precise and shared definitions.

P6: Variation

Distinguishes Common vs. Special cause variation.

P7: Systems Theory

Views organizations as dynamic, interdependent systems.

Performance Frameworks

Assurance vs. Improvement

Quality Assurance (QA) is backward-looking. It asks, "Did we meet the minimum standard?" It focuses on accountability.

Quality Improvement (QI) is forward-looking. It asks, "How can we do this better tomorrow?" It focuses on learning and process refinement.
