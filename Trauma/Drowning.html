Simulated Patient Scenario

Paediatric Drowning

Scenario Details

Scenario: Paediatric Drowning - Cardiac Arrest
Simulated Patient: 4-year-old Child Mannequin
Actor/Actress: Parent (highly distressed)

MDT Information

  • Urgent Response - Drowning / Cardiac Arrest
  • 4-year-old child pulled from backyard swimming pool
  • Unresponsive, not breathing
  • Bystander CPR reportedly in progress (quality unknown)
  • Location: Residential address
  • Police responding

On Arrival

You arrive at a suburban house. A highly distressed parent meets you, pointing towards the backyard pool area. You see a small child lying supine on the poolside paving. Another adult (neighbour/bystander) is performing chest compressions, appearing panicked and possibly ineffective. The child is cyanotic, wet, and appears lifeless. The pool area is fenced but the gate is open.

Scene Safety Note: Ensure pool area is safe, be mindful of wet surfaces. Manage distressed bystanders calmly.

Physical Examination (During Resuscitation)

  • General Appearance: Unresponsive, apneic, pulseless, cyanotic, wet, cold.
  • Airway: Check for foreign material, water, vomit. Froth may be present at mouth/nose.
  • Breathing: No spontaneous effort. Assess chest rise/fall with BVM ventilation.
  • Circulation: Confirm absence of central pulse. Assess skin signs.
  • Neurological: GCS 3. Fixed, dilated pupils common.
  • Exposure: Remove wet clothing rapidly. Check for signs of trauma (especially head/neck if fall suspected).

Assessment and Treatment (Paediatric ALS for Drowning)

Appropriate Management Focus

  • Safety & Scene Management: Ensure rescuer safety. Remove patient from water. Manage bystanders/family supportively.
  • Immediate Resuscitation (DRSABCD): Confirm unresponsiveness, check for breathing/pulse. Commence high-quality CPR immediately (Rate 100-120/min, Depth 1/3 chest diameter ~5cm, full recoil, minimise interruptions). 15:2 compression:ventilation ratio (two rescuers).
  • Airway Management: Open airway (head-tilt/chin-lift or jaw thrust if trauma suspected). Suction airway clear of water/vomit/froth. Insert oropharyngeal airway (OPA) or nasopharyngeal airway (NPA) if tolerated/needed.
  • Breathing/Ventilation: Provide positive pressure ventilation with Bag-Valve-Mask (BVM) using 100% oxygen. Focus on effective chest rise. Consider advanced airway (LMA/ETT) early if BVM ineffective or prolonged resuscitation anticipated, performed by appropriately skilled provider.
  • Circulation/CPR: Continue high-quality CPR. Apply defibrillator pads/monitor ASAP. Analyse rhythm. Follow Paediatric ALS algorithm:
    • Non-Shockable (Asystole/PEA): Continue CPR, gain IV/IO access, administer Adrenaline (10mcg/kg IV/IO) every 3-5 minutes. Identify and treat reversible causes (Hypoxia, Hypothermia, Hypo/Hyperkalaemia, Hypoglycaemia, Hypovolemia, Tamponade, Tension pneumo, Toxins, Thrombosis).
    • Shockable (VF/Pulseless VT): Defibrillate immediately (4 J/kg), resume CPR immediately for 2 mins, analyse rhythm. If still shockable, defibrillate again, resume CPR, administer Adrenaline (10mcg/kg IV/IO) after 2nd shock (then every 3-5 mins). Consider Amiodarone (5mg/kg IV/IO) after 3rd shock.
  • Hypothermia Management: Remove wet clothing. Dry patient thoroughly. Cover with warm blankets. Use active warming measures if available (e.g., forced air warmer, warmed IV fluids - use caution).
  • Glucose Management: Check BGL. Treat hypoglycaemia (<3.0-3.5 mmol/L depending on protocol) with IV Dextrose (e.g., 2-5mL/kg Dextrose 10%) or IM Glucagon if no IV/IO access.
  • Cervical Spine: Maintain manual in-line stabilisation only if significant trauma is suspected based on mechanism (e.g., dive into shallow water, fall from height into water). Routine C-spine immobilisation not recommended in simple drowning.
  • Gastric Decompression: Consider nasogastric/orogastric tube insertion by skilled provider to decompress stomach (improves ventilation), especially if prolonged BVM used.
  • Transport: Rapid transport to the nearest appropriate Emergency Department (preferably with paediatric critical care capabilities) once initial resuscitation is underway or if ROSC achieved. Continue full resuscitation during transport. Pre-notify hospital early (Paediatric drowning arrest, rhythm, interventions, ETA).
  • Family Support: Communicate updates calmly and compassionately to parent/family. Assign a team member for family liaison if possible.
  • Teamwork & Communication: Clear roles, closed-loop communication, adherence to ALS algorithms.

Debrief Focus Points

  • Recognizing drowning and initiating resuscitation promptly.
  • Key differences in drowning resuscitation (emphasis on oxygenation/ventilation).
  • High-quality paediatric CPR techniques (rate, depth, ratio).
  • Airway management in drowning (clearing airway, BVM technique, advanced airway considerations).
  • Paediatric ALS algorithm application (rhythm analysis, drug doses - Adrenaline, Amiodarone, defibrillation energy - 4J/kg).
  • Management of hypothermia in drowning.
  • Importance of checking and correcting hypoglycaemia.
  • Considerations for C-spine immobilisation in drowning.
  • Teamwork, communication, and family support during paediatric resuscitation.
  • Importance of rapid transport and pre-notification.
