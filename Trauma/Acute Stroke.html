Physical Examination (Focused Neuro Exam / Stroke Screen)

  • **Facial Droop:** Present on Left (ask patient to smile/show teeth).
  • **Arm Drift:** Left arm drifts downwards rapidly when held out; Right arm holds position.
  • **Leg Weakness:** Left leg weak against resistance/gravity; Right leg normal strength.
  • **Speech:** Dysarthria (slurred) and/or Aphasia (difficulty finding words/understanding).
  • **Vision:** May report blurred vision or visual field defect (hemianopia) - Assess via confrontation if possible.
  • **Grip Strength:** Weak on Left, Normal on Right.
  • **Sensation:** May be reduced on Left side (assess light touch).
  • **Coordination:** Difficult to assess due to weakness, but likely impaired on Left.
  • **(Use local stroke screening tool e.g., FAST, MASS, RACE, etc.)**

Assessment and Treatment

Appropriate Management

  • Recognise signs/symptoms of acute stroke. **Time critical emergency.**
  • Confirm time last seen well (critical for thrombolysis/clot retrieval).
  • Primary survey (ABCDE), ensure airway patency. Consider positioning (semi-upright if tolerated and airway secure).
  • Perform focused neurological assessment / stroke screen (e.g., FAST/MASS).
  • Obtain vital signs, including BGL (rule out hypoglycaemia as mimic).
  • Oxygen therapy ONLY if SpO2 < 94%. Avoid routine high-flow oxygen.
  • Establish IV access (large bore cannula, preferably antecubital fossa on unaffected side - Right arm). Avoid excessive attempts.
  • Do NOT administer Aspirin or other antiplatelets/anticoagulants pre-hospital unless directed by specific protocol/receiving facility.
  • Manage blood pressure according to local stroke guidelines (often permissive hypertension is allowed unless extremely high or specific contraindications exist). Avoid rapid lowering unless indicated (e.g., >220/120 or specific circumstances).
  • Keep patient Nil By Mouth (NBM) due to risk of aspiration.
  • Minimise on-scene time. Rapid transport is crucial.
  • **Pre-notify receiving hospital (Stroke Alert):** Provide age, gender, time last seen well, key neurological findings (stroke screen results), vital signs (esp. BP, BGL), ETA.
  • Transport patient safely, minimising movement, continuous monitoring en route.
  • Provide reassurance to patient and spouse.
