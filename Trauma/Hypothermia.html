Simulated Patient Scenario

Hypothermia (Elderly Fall)

Scenario Details

Scenario: Elderly patient found outdoors after a fall, presenting with confusion and signs of hypothermia.
Simulated Patient: 80-year-old Male Mannequin or Actor
Actor/Actress: Neighbour (found patient, provides limited info).

MDT Information

  • Non-urgent response initially (Welfare Check), upgraded - Fall / Exposure
  • 80-year-old male found lying in garden by neighbour
  • Patient confused, cold to touch
  • Unknown duration of exposure
  • Neighbour present

On Arrival

Paramedics arrive on a cool, overcast morning (ambient temp ~10Â°C). The patient is found lying on his side on damp grass in the back garden, partially covered by a thin blanket provided by the neighbour. He appears pale and is shivering weakly. The neighbour reports finding him ~20 minutes ago when checking on him, unsure how long he's been there but possibly overnight after a fall.

Physical Examination (Focused)

  • **General:** Lethargic, confused, shivering (may be weak or absent if severe <32Â°C), cold to touch.
  • **Skin:** Pale/ashen/cyanotic, cold. Check for frostbite (unlikely at 10Â°C ambient unless wet/prolonged).
  • **Neurology:** Altered LOC, slurred speech, ataxia/poor coordination if able to move, muscle rigidity.
  • **Cardiovascular:** Bradycardia, hypotension (or normal BP), weak pulses.
  • **Respiratory:** Bradypnoea, shallow breathing.
  • **Trauma Assessment:** Perform head-to-toe assessment carefully (minimising movement) to identify injuries from fall (e.g., head injury, hip fracture, other fractures).

Assessment and Treatment

Appropriate Management

  • Recognise Moderate Hypothermia (<35Â°C, e.g., 32-34Â°C) secondary to environmental exposure after a fall. Consider underlying medical conditions/frailty as contributing factors. **Potential for cardiac instability.**
  • Primary survey (ABCDE), manage immediate life threats. **Handle patient gently** - rough handling can precipitate VF.
  • **Airway:** Maintain patent airway. Consider basic adjuncts if needed.
  • **Breathing:** Provide supplemental oxygen (warmed and humidified if possible) via mask to maintain SpO2 â¥94%. Assist ventilations with BVM if RR severely depressed or inadequate tidal volume.
  • **Circulation:**
    • Continuous ECG monitoring. Be vigilant for dysrhythmias (esp. AF, VF).
    • IV Access: Establish IV access if possible (may be difficult).
    • **Fluid Therapy:** Administer **warmed** IV crystalloid fluids (e.g., Sodium Chloride 0.9% heated to ~40Â°C). Give cautiously, especially if heart failure history suspected. Primarily for volume expansion if hypotensive and to aid core rewarming.
    • Treat hypotension primarily via rewarming. Vasopressors generally avoided unless refractory hypotension persists after initial rewarming/fluids.
    • If VF/VT occurs, standard defibrillation attempts (may be less effective at low core temps). Follow local hypothermic cardiac arrest protocols (may involve limited shocks, delayed drugs, focus on rewarming).
  • **Rewarming:** **PRIORITY**.
    • **Remove wet clothing** immediately.
    • **Passive external rewarming:** Cover with warm, dry blankets (including head). Use space blanket/hypothermia wrap. Prevent further heat loss.
    • **Active external rewarming:** Apply warming blankets (e.g., Bair Hugger) or heat packs to trunk (axillae, groin, torso) - avoid direct skin contact, avoid peripheral rewarming initially (can cause afterdrop).
    • **Minimally invasive core rewarming:** Warmed IV fluids. Warmed humidified oxygen.
  • **Disability:** Check BGL, treat hypoglycaemia if present. Maintain spinal precautions if trauma suspected.
  • **Handle Gently:** Avoid unnecessary or rough movement.
  • Transport urgently to hospital capable of managing hypothermia and potential underlying trauma/medical issues. Minimise heat loss during transport.
  • **Pre-notify:** **Hypothermia Alert** to receiving hospital. Provide age, circumstances (fall, exposure time), **core temperature**, vital signs (confirming bradycardia, bradypnoea, hypotension), GCS, ECG findings (Osborn waves?), suspected injuries, rewarming measures initiated, ETA.
  • Documentation: Detailed record of circumstances, core temperature readings, vital signs trends, ECG findings, neurological status, rewarming methods used, fluid volumes, response to treatment.
