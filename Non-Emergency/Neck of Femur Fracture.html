Physical Examination

  • Right Lower Limb: Obvious shortening and external rotation compared to the left leg. Significant tenderness on palpation over the greater trochanter and inguinal region. Unable to lift leg or tolerate passive movement.
  • Neurovascular Status (Right Foot): Distal pulses (dorsalis pedis/posterior tibial) palpable and strong. Capillary refill < 3 seconds. Sensation intact to light touch. Able to wiggle toes slightly when prompted (assess motor function cautiously).
  • Head-to-Toe: Check for other injuries associated with the fall (head injury, wrist fracture, spinal tenderness). No other obvious injuries noted on initial assessment.

Assessment and Treatment

Appropriate Management

  • Primary survey (ABCDE) and immediate life threats.
  • Effective teamwork and communication (with partner, patient, family). Use clinical pauses.
  • Thorough history taking (AMPLE) from patient and family member.
  • Systematic assessment including head-to-toe examination and neurovascular assessment of the affected limb.
  • Obtain and monitor vital signs frequently.
  • Pain Management: Prioritise early and effective analgesia.
    • Consider Methoxyflurane (if not contraindicated).
    • IV Paracetamol.
    • IV access establishment.
    • Consider IV opioids (e.g., Fentanyl titrated dose, or Morphine) cautiously due to age, frailty, and cognitive impairment. Note Apixaban use - low risk for IV opioids but relevant if considering regional blocks/surgery.
    • (If protocols allow and trained: Consider Fascia Iliaca Compartment Block).
  • Immobilisation: Keep patient comfortable. Support the injured leg with pillows/blankets in the position found. Avoid unnecessary movement. Do NOT apply traction splint. Consider vacuum splint if available and tolerated for extrication.
  • Fluid Therapy: IV fluids if hypotensive or signs of shock, administer cautiously to avoid fluid overload. Maintenance fluids may be appropriate depending on transport time.
  • Maintain patient warmth (blanket).
  • Pre-notify receiving hospital (potential #NOF, elderly, anticoagulated, relevant history).
  • Careful extrication minimising movement of the hip (e.g., scoop stretcher).
  • Ongoing reassessment of vital signs, pain score, and neurovascular status.
  • Clear communication and reassurance provided to the patient and son/daughter.
